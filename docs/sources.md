# Clinical sources (speech-language pathology)

The dialogue is built on real SLP / orthophonie evidence, indexed into the project's
knowledge graph. The session behavior maps directly to these.

- **Health-agency guidance.** NICE guideline NG97 (UK) and HAS + FNO (France). They frame
  screening, the role of speech therapy in the care path, and which non-drug therapies are
  recommended (CST first-line, reminiscence second) or to be avoided.
- **Professional bodies.** ASHA (Practice Portal "Dementia" + Evidence Maps, USA) and RCSLT
  (Dementia Guidance Position Paper, UK): clinical scope, assessment, early person-centered
  intervention.
- **Evidence-based interventions.** Spaced Retrieval Training + Errorless Learning (SRT/EL),
  Semantic Feature Analysis (SFA) for word-finding, Reminiscence Therapy (RT), Cognitive
  Stimulation Therapy (CST/iCST). Each has its own session mechanics and evidence level,
  which structure what Cognitive Companion says and does per scenario.
- **Meta-analyses and systematic reviews.** Cochrane review of reminiscence (Woods et al.,
  2018, 22 RCTs) and the CST meta-analysis (Cafferata et al., 2021, Psychological Bulletin,
  44 RCTs): to anchor real effect sizes and known limits (e.g. effects not durable long-term).
- **Clinical metrics and guardrails.** The SLP measurement grid (lexical recall and latency,
  sequencing, fluency in words/minute, engagement) plus the non-negotiable safety rules:
  errorless learning, validation therapy (never correct a false belief), never set the
  patient up to fail, and an explicit refusal of decontextualized cognitive training that
  NICE/HAS advise against.

Source synthesis: "Orthophonie et Démence : Cadre et Pratiques" (deep-research dossier),
which compiles the citations above.
